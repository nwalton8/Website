Film has been one of my greatest passions since I was a kid sneaking into the living room to watch late-night movies. There's something miraculous about a well-made film — the way light, sound, performance, and story combine to create an experience that stays with you for years. I'm a nursing student by day, but I'm a cinephile at heart. This page is my little corner of the internet dedicated to the movies that have shaped how I see the world.

Browse my all-time favorites, explore how Hollywood's best perform at the box office, and test your movie knowledge in the film trivia app below!

All-Time Favorites

The Shawshank Redemption

1994 Drama

A quietly devastating study in hope, friendship, and the indomitable human spirit. Darabont's masterpiece gets better every time.

Inception

2010 Sci-Fi

Nolan's dream-within-a-dream thriller is endlessly rewatchable. Few blockbusters demand this much from their audience.

Inside Out

2015 Animation

Pixar's most emotionally intelligent film. As someone studying healthcare, the portrayal of emotional complexity hits differently.

"Every passing minute is another chance to turn it all around." — Vanilla Sky (2001)

My Lists

  1. Top Dramatic Performances
    • Tom Hanks — Cast Away (2000)
    • Anthony Hopkins — The Silence of the Lambs (1991)
    • Cate Blanchett — Tár (2022)
    • Daniel Day-Lewis — There Will Be Blood (2007)
  2. Best Cinematography I've Witnessed
    • Blade Runner 2049 — Roger Deakins's opus
    • 1917 — Deakins again, impossibly good
    • Roma — Alfonso Cuarón's personal black-and-white poem
  3. Films That Changed How I Think
    • Wit (2001) — A nurse and a dying English professor. Watch it once and you'll think about it for years.
    • The Diving Bell and the Butterfly — Locked-in syndrome, told from inside.
    • Patch Adams — Corny? Sure. But it got me thinking about healthcare differently.
  4. Comfort Watches (On Repeat)
    • The Grand Budapest Hotel
    • Knives Out
    • La La Land
